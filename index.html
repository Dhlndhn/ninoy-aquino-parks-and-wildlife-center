About Us

Welcome to the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center, your gateway to nature in the heart of Quezon City!

Managed by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR), the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center (NAPWC) is a 23.85-hectare ecological sanctuary that serves as a haven for Philippine flora and fauna. Established in 1954, it is named in honour of the late Senator Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr., reflecting his advocacy for environmental preservation.

Located just a stone’s throw away from Metro Manila's bustling urban life, the park offers a serene retreat where visitors can reconnect with nature. The center is home to diverse ecosystems, including a man-made lagoon, tree-lined paths, and open green spaces that showcase the country’s rich biodiversity.

The NAPWC is more than just a park—it is a commitment to preserving the natural heritage of the Philippines. Whether you're a nature lover, a conservation enthusiast, or simply seeking a peaceful escape, the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center invites you to explore, learn, and contribute to the conservation of our environment.

Explore Nature

Discover the perfect blend of creativity and adventure with Litratour, an engaging activity designed for photography enthusiasts and nature lovers at the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center. White-Bellied Sea Eagle
A powerful raptor with a striking white underbelly and dark wings, the white-bellied sea eagle is found in coastal areas throughout Southeast Asia. It preys on fish, birds, and small mammals and is known for its soaring flight and keen eyesight.
Brahminy kite / Haliastur indus intermedius
A majestic bird of prey, the Brahminy kite is easily recognized by its striking chestnut-colored body and white head. Known for its keen hunting skills, this raptor is often found near coastal areas, preying on fish and small animals.
Burmese rock python / Python bivittatus
One of the largest snake species in the world, the Burmese rock python can grow up to 20 feet in length. Native to Southeast Asia, it is a non-venomous constrictor that hunts small mammals, birds, and reptiles.
Cockatiel / Nymphicus hollandicus
A beloved pet bird with a distinct crest on its head and a cheerful, whistling call, the cockatiel is native to Australia. It is known for its playful personality and beautiful plumage, which often includes shades of grey, white, and yellow.
Common palm civet / Paradoxurus hermaphroditus philippinensis
A nocturnal mammal with a raccoon-like appearance, the common palm civet is known for its ability to climb trees and forage for fruits, insects, and small vertebrates. It plays a crucial role in seed dispersal in its habitat.
Green-cheeked conure / Pyrrhura molinae
A small, colorful parrot native to South America, the green-cheeked conure is known for its playful, affectionate nature. Its vivid green feathers and cheerful demeanor make it a popular companion bird.
Iguana
A large, herbivorous reptile with a distinctive long tail and spiny crest running down its back, the iguana is native to Central and South America. It is an excellent climber and can often be found basking in the sun to regulate its body temperature.
Lar gibbon / Hylobates lar
This agile primate is known for its long arms and impressive acrobatic abilities. Native to Southeast Asia, lar gibbons are primarily arboreal and communicate with a range of vocalizations, including loud calls that can carry for miles.
Nicobar pigeon / Caloenas nicobarica nicobarica
Native to the Nicobar Islands, this striking pigeon is known for its iridescent feathers and distinctive greenish-blue sheen. The Nicobar pigeon plays an important role in forest ecology by dispersing seeds as it forages for fruits.
Philippine long-tailed macaque / Macaca fascicularis philippensis
A subspecies of the macaque found throughout the Philippines, this intelligent and social primate has a long tail and a wide variety of vocalizations. It is highly adaptable and often found in forests, as well as urban areas.
Red-eared slider / Trachemys scripta elegans
This freshwater turtle is easily identified by the distinctive red markings around its ears. Native to the United States, the red-eared slider is a popular pet and can often be seen basking in the sun along riverbanks or ponds.
Rose-ringed parakeet / Psittacula krameri
A striking green parrot with a distinct rose-colored ring around its neck, the rose-ringed parakeet is native to Africa and South Asia. Known for its intelligence and ability to mimic human speech, it is a popular companion bird.
Rufous Hornbill
A large, majestic bird with a vivid orange and black plumage, the rufous hornbill is known for its striking casque (the large structure atop its beak) and its ability to produce loud, resonant calls. It is an important species in Southeast Asian rainforests.
Saltwater Crocodile
The largest living reptile, the saltwater crocodile is a formidable predator that can grow over 23 feet long. Found in coastal areas across Southeast Asia, it is known for its strength, speed, and ability to thrive in both saltwater and freshwater environments.
Tiger / Panthera tigris
A symbol of strength and beauty, the tiger is one of the most iconic big cats in the world. With its orange coat marked by black stripes, the tiger is a powerful predator native to Asia and is known for its solitary nature and impressive hunting skills.
